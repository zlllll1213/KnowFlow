# Product

## Register

product

## Users

KnowFlow is used by people who need to turn internal documents into a searchable, cited knowledge base. Primary users are team members, operators, analysts, technical staff, and administrators who upload documents, monitor parsing status, ask questions against a chosen knowledge base, and verify answers through source chunks and Agent traces.

## Product Purpose

KnowFlow exists to make document knowledge usable after upload: parse files, split and embed content, retrieve relevant chunks, generate answers through RAG or Agent mode, and show enough provenance for users to trust or reject the answer. Success means users can move from document upload to a cited answer without guessing whether the system is working.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, technical. The product should feel like a dependable AI operations console: clear status, low drama, strong evidence, and fast paths to the next action.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing-page tropes, decorative glass cards, fake controls for unsupported backend features, noisy cyberpunk dashboards, one-note blue gradients, oversized empty cards, tiny low-contrast metadata, and interfaces that imply sources or workflow telemetry when the current API cannot provide them.

## Design Principles

1. Show system state before decoration: parsing, retrieval, confidence, and citations must be easier to see than visual effects.
2. Keep the workflow honest: every visible control should map to an existing route or API, or be clearly omitted.
3. Make evidence legible: source chunks, file names, scores, and Agent steps should be scannable under pressure.
4. Optimize for repeated use: dense but calm layouts, stable dimensions, keyboard-friendly paths, and responsive views that do not hide the main task.
5. Use motion sparingly to explain state changes, never to distract from reading or verification.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus states, reduced-motion preferences, readable Chinese UI copy, and responsive layouts that avoid horizontal overflow on common laptop and mobile widths.
